Step-by-Step UI Walkthrough: How to Test Each Module

Follow this click-by-click guide to test the system exactly as a real user would.

------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 1: LOGGING IN
------------------------------------------------------
1. Open your browser and navigate to your local frontend URL (usually `http://localhost:5173`).
2. You will see the Login screen.
3. Click on the Email input field and enter your email.
4. Click on the Password field and enter your password.
   (Tip: Log in with the "Super Admin" account first so you can see all modules).
5. Press the "Login" button.
6. You will instantly be redirected to the main Dashboard where you can see high-level statistics and alerts.

------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 2: USER & ROLE MANAGEMENT
------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Add staff members like Customs Officers or Shipping Agents to the system.
1. On the left-hand sidebar menu, click on "User Management" or "Staff".
2. To add someone new:
   - Click the "Add User" or "Register Staff" button on the top right.
   - A form will pop up. Click and type in the Name, Email, and a temporary Password.
   - Click the "Role" dropdown and select "Customs Officer".
   - Press the "Save" or "Create" button.
3. You will now see the new user added to the staff list table.

------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 3: CREATING NEW CARGO (BOOKING)
------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Log a new physical shipment into the database.
1. On the left sidebar menu, click on "Cargo Management", "Shipments", or "Logistics".
2. Click the "Add New Cargo" or "Create Shipment" button.
3. A form will appear. Fill in the required details:
   - Type the Consignee (Client Name).
   - Type the Cargo Type (e.g., Electronics, Agricultural).
   - Enter Weight and Destination.
4. Press "Submit" or "Save Cargo".
5. The table will refresh showing your new cargo. 
6. IMPORTANT: Look at the "Cargo ID" or "Tracking Number" column. Copy or write down this ID for the next steps!

------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 4: CUSTOMS CLEARANCE & AUDIT
------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Approve the cargo so it is legally allowed to leave the port.
1. On the left sidebar menu, click on "Customs Clearance".
2. You will see a list of shipments. Find the cargo you just created. It will have a blue "PENDING VERIFICATION" badge.
3. Click the blue "Process Review" button located on the far right of that cargo's row.
4. The "Customs Audit Panel" pop-up will appear on your screen.
5. Under "Verification Determination", click the dropdown and select "Release Authorized (Approve & Invoice)".
6. Click into the "Inspector Remarks" text box and type: "Documents verified, phytosanitary seals checked."
7. Press the blue "Sign and Apply" button.
8. The pop-up will close, and you will see the badge instantly change to a green "CUSTOMS CLEARED".

------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 5: UPDATING SHIPPING STATUS (DISPATCH)
------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Move the cargo physically and update its status in the system.
1. Go back to the "Cargo Management" or "Shipments" page on the sidebar.
2. Find your cleared Cargo and click the "Update Status" button (or click the edit pencil icon).
3. A status window will open. Change the dropdown from "Pending" to "In Transit".
4. Press "Save Status". (This simulates the truck leaving the port).
5. Once the truck arrives at the destination, click "Update Status" again.
6. Change the dropdown to "Delivered" and press "Save Status".

------------------------------------------------------
MODULE 6: CLIENT TRACKING (THE CUSTOMER VIEW)
------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: See what the customer sees when they want to know where their package is.
1. Click your profile icon at the top right of the screen and press "Logout".
2. If your app has a public "Track Package" page on the home screen, stay there. (Otherwise, log in with a "Client" account and go to "Track Cargo").
3. Click on the Tracking Search box.
4. Type or paste the unique Cargo ID/Tracking Number you wrote down earlier.
5. Press the "Track" or "Search" button.
6. You will see a timeline display on the screen showing the entire history: 
   - Booked -> Customs Cleared -> In Transit -> Delivered.
