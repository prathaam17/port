NMPA Port Inventory Management System: Working Process, Flow, and Role Guide

=========================================
1. WHAT EACH ROLE DOES (IN SIMPLE WORDS)
=========================================

* SUPER ADMIN (The System Administrator)
  - Purpose: Full top-down control over the entire software system.
  - Key Tasks:
    - Creates and manages user accounts for port staff.
    - Inspects system-wide security audit logs tracking every user's actions.
    - Monitors global terminal analytics (cargo throughput, total revenue, warehouse load).

* PORT OPERATIONS OFFICER (The Dock Supervisor)
  - Purpose: Coordinates the physical arrival of vessels at the port berths.
  - Key Tasks:
    - Oversees the arrival of incoming ships (vessels).
    - Unloads cargo container lists from vessels.
    - Marks cargo status as "Unloaded" once they are safely placed on the port docks.

* WAREHOUSE MANAGER (The Storage Coordinator)
  - Purpose: Manages storage space in the port warehouses and yard zones.
  - Key Tasks:
    - Checks space capacity inside container yards and cargo sheds.
    - Allocates physical coordinates/slots (Yard Zones/Storage Bays) for newly unloaded cargo.
    - Generates storage inventory records to track cargo weight allocations.

* CUSTOMS OFFICER (The Border Control / Inspector)
  - Purpose: Reviews and audits cargo manifest documents for regulatory compliance.
  - Key Tasks:
    - Audits cargo manifest PDFs and Bill of Lading certificates.
    - Schedules physical inspections if documents are suspicious.
    - Grants or Denies customs clearance (Status: "Customs Approved" or "Customs Hold").

* FINANCE OFFICER (The Billing & Accounts Manager)
  - Purpose: Manages storage invoice generation and payment collections.
  - Key Tasks:
    - Generates invoice bills for cargo storage fees.
    - Calculates penalties (demurrage) if cargo stays longer than the 3-day free tier limit.
    - Settle billing payments (cash, card, bank transfer) to release the financial holds on cargo.

* GATE OFFICER (The Security & Weighbridge Operator)
  - Purpose: Controls physical security entry/exit gates and weighbridge scales.
  - Key Tasks:
    - Verifies driver licenses and license plates of transport trucks.
    - Scans truck QR codes (Gate Passes) at the port gate.
    - Checks in trucks upon entry and logs final weighbridge dispatch on exit.

* SHIPPING AGENT (The Logistics Partner / Customer Representative)
  - Purpose: Represents the companies importing or exporting cargo at the port.
  - Key Tasks:
    - Uploads initial cargo manifest lists for arriving ships.
    - Checks invoice balances and pays cargo storage bills online.
    - Generates QR Gate Passes to dispatch cleared cargo onto delivery trucks.

=========================================
2. THE STEP-BY-STEP WORKING PROCESS & FLOW
=========================================

The lifecycle of cargo moving through the New Mangalore Port Authority (NMPA) terminal:

Step 1: Manifest Registration
- The Shipping Agent uploads the vessel's cargo manifest (consignee, type, weight, and manifest file).
- The cargo enters the database with status "Manifest Uploaded" and triggers a "Pending Verification" customs log.

Step 2: Vessel Berth & Cargo Unloading
- When the ship berths at the dock, the Port Operations Officer records the unloading of cargo.
- The status changes to "Unloaded".

Step 3: Yard Zone Allocation
- The Warehouse Manager picks a warehouse/container yard with sufficient capacity and assigns a storage location.
- The status updates to "Allocated", space is deducted from the warehouse, and a live inventory record is created.

Step 4: Customs Review & Approval
- The Customs Officer examines the attached documents, schedules an inspection date, and selects "Approved".
- The status updates to "Customs Approved".
- Crucially, this approval automatically triggers the creation of a billing Invoice inside the database.

Step 5: Billing Settle
- The Finance Officer processes the billing. If the cargo stayed past the 3-day free limit, demurrage penalties are calculated.
- Once payment is settled, the invoice status changes to "Paid".

Step 6: Gate Pass Issuance
- The Shipping Agent registers a truck and driver details to collect the cargo.
- If Customs is approved AND the Invoice is Paid, the system generates a secure QR Gate Pass.
- The cargo status becomes "Gate Pass Generated".

Step 7: Weighbridge Gate Operations (Check-In & Dispatch)
- The truck arrives at the entrance gate. The Gate Officer scans the QR Gate Pass and authorizes entry. (Status: "Entered").
- The truck is loaded with the cargo. On exit, the Gate Officer scans it again to verify weighbridge details and dispatches it. (Status: "Dispatched").
- The system automatically frees up storage capacity in the warehouse/yard and destroys the active inventory record.

3. STRUCTURE OF HOW PROCESSES TAKE PLACE IN POSTGRESQL
=========================================

All actions trigger a relational database transaction via Sequelize ORM:

1. User Action in React Frontend
   - Example: Finance Officer clicks "Collect Fee" on Invoice #INV-NMPA-101.
2. Express Router Matches URL
   - A PUT request is sent to `http://localhost:5000/api/billing/invoice/:id/pay`.
3. Middleware Verifies Role
   - Verifies the user is logged in (JWT validation) and has the "Finance" or "Super Admin" role.
4. Controller Updates DB
   - The billingController updates the Invoice row in the PostgreSQL `Invoices` table: `status = 'Paid'`.
   - It writes a new transaction entry into the PostgreSQL `Payments` table.
5. PostgreSQL Syncs Data
   - PostgreSQL executes the foreign-key updates and returns the successful write confirmation.
6. Feedback to Client
   - Backend sends a JSON response. React triggers a UI toast alert: "Payment Succeeded" and refreshes the table rows.
